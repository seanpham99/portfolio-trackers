## 2. T+2 SETTLEMENT CYCLE

### 2.1 Settlement Timeline

Vietnam operates a **T+2 settlement cycle** for equities:

| Day                | Action                                        | Timing                           |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **T (Trade Date)** | Order execution                               | During trading hours             |
| **T+1**            | Depository members confirm funds availability | By 4:45 PM                       |
| **T+2**            | VSD and clearing banks complete payment       | 11:30 AM - 12:30 PM              |
| **T+2**            | Securities available in investor accounts     | By 12:30 PM                      |
| **T+2**            | Investors can trade received securities       | Afternoon session (1:00-2:30 PM) |

**Key Improvement (Effective August 29, 2022):**  
Settlement completion moved from 3:30-4:00 PM to 11:30 AM-12:30 PM on T+2, allowing same-day trading of settled securities.

**Sources:**

- HSC Notice (Decision 110/QD-VSD, Aug 19, 2022)
- Global Referral Network (Decision 48/QÄ-HÄTV/2024 of VSD)
- Vietnam News article on T+2 settlement improvements
- BSC Weekly Report May 2025

**Confidence:** **VERIFIED**

### 2.2 Settlement Infrastructure

**Vietnam Securities Depository (VSD)** manages the central depository and clearing system:

- Electronic book-entry transfer
- Dematerialized securities (no physical certificates)
- Multilateral netting system
- Settlement through designated clearing banks

**Bond Settlement:** T+1 cycle (faster than equities)

**Sources:** VSD official website, Global Referral Network  
**Confidence:** **HIGH**

### 2.3 Settlement Requirements

**For Domestic Investors:**

- Cash must be available by T+2
- Securities companies may offer credit facilities
- Failed settlements subject to penalties and forced sell-out

**For Foreign Investors (MAJOR CHANGE - November 2, 2024):**

**Previous System (Before Nov 2, 2024):**

- **100% pre-funding required** before order placement
- Cash deposited with custodian/broker before trading
- Exposure to FX risk regardless of transaction completion

**Current System (Since Nov 2, 2024):**

- **No pre-funding required for foreign institutional investors**
- Securities firms assess client financial capacity
- Firms determine margin requirements by mutual agreement
- If insufficient funds, securities firm covers shortfall and must sell securities immediately upon receipt
- Custodian banks responsible for covering shortfalls if they incorrectly confirm account balances

**Legal Framework:**

- **Circular 68/2024/TT-BTC** (issued Sept 18, 2024, effective Nov 2, 2024)
- **Circular 18/2025/TT-BTC** (issued April 26, 2025)

**Impact:** This removal of pre-funding is a critical milestone toward Vietnam's market upgrade to emerging status.

**Sources:**

- Vietnam Briefing (Nov 7, 2024)
- Vietnam News (Sept 2024)
- Hanoi Times (Sept 19, 2024)

- The Shiv (Oct 2025)
- The Investor (Sept 12, 2025)

### 4.2 Order Types

**Available Order Types:**

1. **Limit Order (LO)**
   - Standard order with specified price
   - Valid until end of trading day or cancellation
   - Price and time priority matching

2. **ATO (At The Opening)**
   - Market order for opening session
   - Priority over limit orders during opening
   - Automatically canceled if not executed
   - **KRX System Change (May 5, 2025):** ATO orders now display at defined price level, no longer show "ATO" marker

3. **ATC (At The Close)**
   - Market order for closing session
   - Used in periodic matching
   - **KRX System Change (May 5, 2025):** ATC orders now display at defined price level

4. **Market Orders (HNX)**
   - **MTL (Market-to-Limit):** Buys at lowest ask/sells at highest bid, remainder converts to limit order
   - **MOK (Match-or-Kill):** Executes immediately or cancels
   - **MAK (Match-and-Kill/Fill-and-Kill):** Can be partially matched, remainder canceled

5. **Stop Orders:** Limited availability (requires broker-specific confirmation)

**Matching Priority:**

1. Price priority (best price executes first)
2. Time priority (earlier orders at same price execute first)

### 4.4 Put-Through Transactions

**Definition:** Pre-negotiated trades between parties executed through exchange system.

**Characteristics:**

- Parties negotiate price and volume in advance
- Executed through exchange but at negotiated terms
- Must be within daily price limits (ceiling/floor range)
- Available during put-through sessions (9:00-11:30 AM, 1:00-3:00 PM)
- **No minimum volume for bonds**
- Lower transaction fees compared to regular matching

**Price Requirements:**

- Must stay within Â±7% (HSX) or Â±10% (HNX) price limit for the day
- Cannot exceed ceiling or floor prices
- No price tick restrictions for put-through (HSX)

**KRX System Changes (May 5, 2025):**

- Both buyer and seller can now input orders (previously only sellers)
- One party confirms the trade
- Executed put-through trades can no longer be canceled or amended

**Common Uses:**

- Large institutional block trades
- Strategic investor transactions
- Transfer between related parties
- Reducing market impact of large orders

**Transparency:**

- Reported to exchange
- Included in daily trading statistics
- Published information requirements

**Concerns:** Potential for price manipulation; subject to regulatory oversight.
